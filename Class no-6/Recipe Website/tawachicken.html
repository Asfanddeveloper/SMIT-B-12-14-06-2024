Tawa Chicken With Nihari







Discriptions

Combining two beloved dishes, Tawa Chicken with Nihari, creates a fusion that tantalizes taste buds. Tawa Chicken is a popular North Indian and Pakistani dish, known for its quick preparation on a flat griddle (tawa) and its spicy, tangy flavor profile. Nihari, on the other hand, is a slow-cooked stew made with meat (usually beef or mutton) and a rich, aromatic gravy, typically enjoyed as a breakfast dish. In this fusion recipe, the succulent pieces of Tawa Chicken are paired with the deep, flavorful gravy of Nihari, creating a unique and satisfying meal that blends the quick-cooking methods and spices of Tawa Chicken with the slow-cooked depth of Nihari.



Ingredients:

For Tawa Chicken:

  • 500g boneless chicken pieces
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

    • For Nihari Gravy:

  • 500g beef or mutton (with bone for extra flavor)
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup yogurt, whisked
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp Nihari masala (available in stores or homemade)
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Freshly sliced ginger (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges

    • Instructions:

    For Tawa Chicken:

  • Heat oil on a tawa (flat griddle) or in a wide pan over medium heat.
  • Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and oil separates.
  • Add the chicken pieces and cook for a few minutes until they are no longer pink.
  • Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook until the chicken is fully cooked and spices are well blended.
  • Sprinkle garam masala and chopped green chilies. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and set aside.

    • For Nihari Gravy:

  • Heat oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  • Add the meat pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  • Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, and Nihari masala. Mix well.
  • Add the whisked yogurt and cook for a few minutes, stirring continuously to prevent curdling.
  • Pour in enough water to cover the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and the gravy has thickened.
  • Add garam masala and salt to taste. Cook for another 10 minutes.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, sliced ginger, and lemon wedges.

    • To Serve:

  • Place a generous portion of Tawa Chicken on a serving plate.
  • Pour a ladleful of Nihari gravy over the Tawa Chicken.
  • Garnish with additional fresh coriander leaves and ginger slices.
  • Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
