Malai-Kofta-Curry






Discriptions

Malai Kofta Curry is a rich and creamy North Indian dish that consists of deep-fried vegetable balls (koftas) simmered in a flavorful and luxurious gravy. This dish is often reserved for special occasions due to its rich taste and decadent nature. The koftas are made with a combination of mashed potatoes, paneer, and various spices, while the curry is a smooth blend of tomatoes, cream, and aromatic spices.


Ingredients:

For Koftas:

  • 2 cups grated paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger, finely grated
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 cup cashews, chopped
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

    • For Gravy:

  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 4 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or malai
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

    • Instructions:

    For Koftas:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine grated paneer, mashed potatoes, cornflour, chopped green chilies, grated ginger, garam masala, and salt.
  • Mix well to form a smooth dough. If the mixture feels too soft, add a bit more cornflour to bind it.
  • Take a small portion of the mixture, flatten it slightly, and place a few chopped cashews and raisins in the center. Roll it into a ball, ensuring the filling is covered completely.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining mixture.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the koftas until they are golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels and set aside.

    • For Gravy:

  • Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, and cardamom pods. Sauté for a minute until aromatic.
  • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  • Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  • Add heavy cream or malai and stir well to combine. Cook for another 5-7 minutes on low heat.
  • Add sugar to balance the flavors. Adjust salt if needed.
  • Gently add the fried koftas to the gravy and simmer for a few minutes to allow the koftas to soak up the flavors.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

    • To Serve:

  • Serve the Malai Kofta Curry hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice.
  • Garnish with additional cream or a dollop of butter for extra richness.
