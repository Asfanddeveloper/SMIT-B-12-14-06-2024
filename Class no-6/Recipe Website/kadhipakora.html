Punjabi-Kadhi-Pakora






Discriptions

Punjabi Kadhi Pakora is a classic North Indian dish that is cherished for its tangy and spicy flavors. This dish consists of crispy pakoras (fried gram flour fritters) simmered in a yogurt and gram flour-based curry, which is seasoned with a variety of aromatic spices. It's typically served with steamed rice or roti, making it a perfect comfort food for many.


Ingredients:

For Pakoras:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil for deep frying

    • For Kadhi:

  • 2 cups yogurt (dahi)
  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds (methi dana)
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

    • Instructions:

    For Pakoras:

  • In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, sliced onions, chopped green chilies, red chili powder, turmeric powder, carom seeds, and salt.
  • Add water gradually to form a thick batter. Mix well so that the onions are well coated with the batter.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  • Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and fry until the pakoras are golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels and set aside.

    • For Kadhi:

  • In a large bowl, whisk together yogurt and gram flour until smooth and no lumps remain.
  • Add water to the mixture and whisk again to combine. Set aside.
  • Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and dried red chilies. Sauté until the seeds crackle and the spices are fragrant.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another minute.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  • Slowly pour in the yogurt-gram flour mixture, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Add the fried pakoras to the kadhi and let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the pakoras to soak up the flavors.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

    • To Serve:

  • Serve the Punjabi Kadhi Pakora hot with steamed rice or roti.
  • Garnish with additional coriander leaves if desired.
